Proof that two-stage reduction by averaging over equal intervals produces the same result as one stage reduction.

`(((sum_(i=1)^(N/2) v_i)/(N/2) + (sum_(i=(N/2+1))^(N/2) v_i)/(N/2)))/2` `= ((sum_(i=1)^N v_i)/(N/2))/2`

`= (((sum_(i=1)^N v_i)/(N/2))/2) * (1/2)/(1/2)` `= ((sum_(i=1)^N v_i)/N)`

Example: 
22+5+7+17+9+2+21+4+18+6 => 111 # total of 10 observations

111.0 / 10 => 11.1  # average over all observations
# split in two equal parts
# first part:
22+5+7+17+9 => 60
60.0 /5 => 12.0     # average over first five observations

# second part:
2+21+4+18+6 => 51
51.0 /5 => 10.2

# average of two averages:
(12.0 + 10.2)/2 => 11.1


Generalize for an interval of w observations.

`(((sum_(i=1)^(N/w) v_i)/(N/w) + (sum_(i=(N/w+1))^(2*w) v_i)/(N/w) + ... + (sum_(i=(N-w))^(N/w) v_i)/(N/w)))/w` `= ((sum_(i=1)^N v_i)/(N/w))/w`

`= (((sum_(i=1)^N v_i)/(N/w))/w) * (1/w)/(1/w)` `= ((sum_(i=1)^N v_i)/N)`
